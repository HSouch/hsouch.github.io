About Me

I spent a significant amount of my life in Halifax Nova Scotia before moving to the United States. I began my university education at Dalhousie University (physics and computer science) before switching to Saint Mary's University to pursue astrophysics. I completed my BSc in Honours Astrophysics in 2020, under the supervision of Dr. Ivana Damjanov.

I was accepted into the Astronomy PhD program at Yale in 2020, but due to personal (and global) reasons decided to defer until September 2021, and now I'm finally in New Haven.

I am an avid badminton player (go Momota!), and I've played soccer for pretty much my entire life as a central-midfielder (go Liverpool!).

I play multiple instruments, primarily bass guitar, but also (regular) guitar, some piano, harmonica, and the melodica. Lately I have been learning a lot of funk and nu-disco . While in New Haven, I have the goal of joining a band to put this experience playing music to work. I used to do a lot of creating writing, and if you want to count winning NaNoWriMo projects as completed works (you shouldn't), then I have written three novels (which will never see the light of day).
